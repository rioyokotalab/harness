# Research presentation workflow

1. Establish audience, duration, venue, desired decision or takeaway, prior
   knowledge, delivery format, and source material.
2. Write the one-sentence thesis and storyboard before polishing slides. Give
   each slide one job and make the narrative dependency between slides explicit.
3. Trace every technical claim, number, figure, and borrowed visual to a source.
   Separate published results, new measurements, intuition, and speculation.
4. Prefer explanatory diagrams and readable plots over dense text. Preserve
   axis labels, units, uncertainty, comparison baselines, color accessibility,
   and legibility at the target room/screen size.
5. Keep detailed derivations, provenance, and contingency material in notes or
   backup slides. Never hide an important limitation merely to simplify the
   story.
6. Export and inspect the actual deliverable at representative resolution.
   Check clipping, fonts, contrast, media, links, equations, animation fallback,
   slide count, and timing; rehearse transitions and likely questions.
7. Deliver the editable source, exported artifact, source ledger, unresolved
   claims, and a concise rehearsal checklist.
