# Research program management

1. Confirm the intended audience and privacy boundary. Keep private student,
   personnel, unpublished, and sponsor-sensitive information out of public or
   shared artifacts unless explicitly authorized.
2. Maintain one compact record per project: objective, owner, collaborators,
   current evidence, milestone, next action, due/decision date, dependencies,
   risks, blockers, and source links. Keep facts separate from interpretation.
3. Derive status from artifacts and explicit updates, not inferred motivation,
   ability, effort, or personal circumstances. Flag stale evidence rather than
   guessing progress.
4. Prepare meetings around decisions and unblockers: changes since last review,
   evidence to inspect, questions requiring judgment, commitments, and the next
   smallest verifiable step.
5. Track cross-project resource conflicts, shared infrastructure, deadlines,
   publication dependencies, and mentorship load without ranking people by
   opaque scores.
6. Record decisions with rationale, owner, review date, and reversal condition.
   Escalate consent, authorship, personnel, safety, and high-impact allocation
   choices to the responsible human.
7. Produce a portfolio view containing evidence-backed state, upcoming
   decisions, risks, and actions. Create reminders or external messages only
   with the required authority.
